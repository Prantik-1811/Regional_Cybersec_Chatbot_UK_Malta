https://www.cybercrowd.co.uk/news/are-your-it-systems-secure-key-it-health-check-ithc-requirements-for-educational-institutions Pen Testing In today’s rapidly evolving digital landscape, ensuring the security and integrity of IT systems in educational institutions is more critical than ever. With the rise of digital threats, the Education and Skills Funding Agency (ESFA) mandates annual IT Health Checks (ITHCs) for further education (FE) institutions as part of their funding agreements. These checks are essential for protecting sensitive data and maintaining robust cybersecurity measures. They ensure that institutions are compliant, their IT estates are visible, external systems are safeguarded against unauthorised access, and internal systems are free from significant weaknesses. The key question is: Is your college equipped to meet these requirements and safeguard its IT estate from potential cyber threats? Why Are IT Health Checks Important? IT Health Checks are essential for identifying vulnerabilities in your IT systems. They help safeguard against unauthorised access and ensure that your internal systems are secure and up to date. Regular checks not only protect sensitive student and staff data but also help manage a diverse and complex IT infrastructure, mitigating risks from unsupported software and unpatched systems. Key Components of the ITHC An IT Health Check encompasses several critical components, including: External Testing : Focuses on systems accessible from outside the institution, such as email servers, web servers, and remote access solutions. Internal Testing : Ensures the integrity of the internal network and systems, including endpoints, servers, and network devices. Wi-Fi Security Assessment : Checks for unauthorised network entry points and ensures secure wireless communications. Firewall Rule Configuration Checks : Verifies that firewall rules are properly set to prevent unauthorised access. Device Build Reviews : Ensures that all devices, including laptops and desktops, are running the latest software versions and patches. Server Estate Assessment : Evaluates the security of both Windows and Linux servers within the institution’s IT estate. How Can IT Health Checks Benefit Your Institution? Immediate Risk Identification : Quickly uncover and address critical vulnerabilities. Enhanced Security Posture : Develop long-term strategies for ongoing protection. Compliance and Funding : Ensure your institution meets ESFA funding requirements.
