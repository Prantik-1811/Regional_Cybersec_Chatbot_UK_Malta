Developing national resilience, internationally. Since its inception in 2019, CYSIAM has delivered sustainable outcomes in >25 countries around the world implementing national and critical infrastructure cyber defence operations capability with the UK's international partners. All of our consultants in this area are technical generalists, but with specialisms, and without exception are seasoned communicators, often having to work with international colleagues through interpreters. We are expeditionary by nature; cultural awareness and diplomacy are core attributes of our highly experienced team.​ Speak to us Our Approach We focus on impactful and sustainable outcomes. Our way of working is through partnership, providing support and challenge when appropriate to enable growth for the international beneficiary’s capability, whilst building the relationships necessary to make the UK safer, stronger, and more prosperous. We add value by demonstrating our technical competence to the critical stakeholders, whilst building trust through our understanding of cultural differences and sensitivities. Our team always maintain focus on the required outcomes in the face of often competing stakeholder views and agendas, and remain tenacious in our drive to get the most benefit out of every situation. Importantly, we collaborate and share knowledge wherever possible to give the UK, its allies, and our clients the best possible chance of staying one step ahead of our adversaries. In 2020, CYSIAM was appointed as an Implementing Partner of the invite-only Global Forum on Cyber Expertise (GFCE) based in The Hague. The typical areas where the CYSIAM team assist in the design, build, or delivery of capability for Critical National Infrastructure (CNI) include: Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) Security Operating Centre (SOC) Computer Incident Response Team (CIRT) National Incident Response Exercises Digital Forensics & Incident Response (DFIR) Compromise Assessments Penetration Testing Benefits Improved Cyber Resilience: Ensures the continuity of essential services but also builds public trust in digital systems, crucial as societies become increasingly reliant on technology. Enhanced National Security: Improves ability for nations to protect critical infrastructure & data from cyber threats, reducing vulnerabilities & safeguarding critical services. Human Development: Supports the advancement of a skilled workforce proficient in cybersecurity, creating job opportunities & promoting a resilient digital economy. Global Collaboration: Builds trusted relationships between countries that share common adversaries, fostering an environment where resources can be combined to enrich intelligence.
