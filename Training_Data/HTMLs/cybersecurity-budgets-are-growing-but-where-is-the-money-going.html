https://www.cybercrowd.co.uk/news/cybersecurity-budgets-are-growing-but-where-is-the-money-going News Infosecurity Europe’s 2025 Cybersecurity Trends Report has revealed a significant shift in cybersecurity investment, with UK organisations predicting an average budget increase of 31% over the next 12 months. While this growth reflects a heightened awareness of cyber risks, the key question becomes: Where is the money going – and is it making a real difference? More Budget, More Security? Not Necessarily Throwing money at cybersecurity won’t automatically make an organisation safer. In fact, many businesses still fall victim to attacks despite major investments – often because funds are directed toward new tools while core fundamentals are overlooked. For example, recent breaches have stemmed not from a lack of tools, but from unpatched software, misconfigured systems , or human error – all of which can fly under the radar in a tech-heavy, strategy-light budget plan. The Foundations of a Smart Cyber Budget A well-rounded, resilient cybersecurity program should focus on three core pillars: 1. Technology – Investing in the Right Tools Yes, automation, threat detection, and endpoint security matter – but only when tools are properly configured, integrated, and supported. Avoid tool sprawl – too many platforms create complexity and blind spots. Prioritise value-add areas like zero-trust architectures and EDR/XDR . Invest in tools that match your risk profile , not just industry hype. 2. People – Strengthening Awareness & Talent Technology can’t stop a user from clicking a phishing link. That’s why investing in people is critical. Fund cyber awareness training , simulations, and behaviour change programs. Upskill internal teams and invest in attracting/retaining cybersecurity talent . Create a culture where cyber risk is owned across the business . 3. Processes – Building Security from the Ground Up Cyber hygiene isn’t glamorous, but it’s essential. Basics like patch management , access control , and MFA need budget attention. Test and refine your incident response and business continuity plans . Conduct regular tabletop exercises to improve reaction under pressure. Where Should You Focus in 2025? CISOs and tech leaders need to think beyond tools and take a holistic approach to spending. Here are five smart budget priorities for the year ahead: Top 5 Budget Priorities for 2025 Security Awareness Training – Still the best ROI in cybersecurity. Zero Trust Implementation – Reduce risk at every access point. Third-Party Risk Management – Secure your supply chain. Incident Response & Recovery – Be ready, not reactive. Tool Consolidation & Integration – Fewer platforms, greater clarity. Strategic Spending Starts with Risk Alignment To get the most out of growing budgets: Align with Business Risk – Focus on the threats that matter to your operations. Engage the Board – Translate cyber risk into business language. Track ROI – Make sure every investment contributes to measurable resilience. Looking Ahead: From Spending More to Spending Smarter With cyber threats evolving rapidly – and the growing influence of AI, insider risk, and regulatory pressure – the future of cyber resilience lies not in the size of your budget, but in how wisely it’s spent . Every pound should strengthen your organisation’s ability to detect, respond to, and recover from attacks. That means balancing tech with people and process – because a well-rounded approach isn’t optional, it’s essential. What do you think? Where should security leaders be focusing their spend in 2025?
