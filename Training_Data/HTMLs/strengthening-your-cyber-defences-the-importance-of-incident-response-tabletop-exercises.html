What Are Incident Response Tabletop Exercises? Tabletop exercises are scenario-based simulations that allow key stakeholders to test their response to a hypothetical cyber incident in a safe, controlled environment. These exercises focus on decision-making, communication, and collaboration, revealing gaps in your processes and helping your team better understand their roles in a crisis. Tabletop vs. Live-Play Exercises: Which One Do You Need? At CyberCrowd, we offer both Tabletop Exercises and Live-Play Simulations , each tailored to meet your needs. Tabletop Exercises These are discussion-based activities where participants work through a simulated scenario step by step. Ideal for testing communication protocols, decision-making, and overall strategy, tabletop exercises are perfect for organisations starting to refine their incident response processes. Use Case: A company wanting to validate its incident response plan without the pressure of a live event. Live-Play Simulations These involve real-time, hands-on scenarios that mimic the chaos of an actual cyber incident. Participants engage with technology and systems in a high-pressure environment, making live-play exercises invaluable for advanced teams or organisations that want to test their operational readiness under stress. Use Case: A critical infrastructure provider ensuring their response team can handle a sophisticated ransomware attack.
